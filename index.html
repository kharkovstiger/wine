Favorable natural conditions of the peninsula contributed to the cultivation of grapes and winemaking since ancient times. The ancient Greeks south-eastern part of the peninsula was called Enotria , what was done, according to some assumptions, the Greek οἶνος , « wine ", as the country was rich in vineyards . Due to the active Greek colonization of the peninsula the ancient wine-making technologies were perceived peoples who inhabited Italy, including the Etruscans.

With the rise of the Roman Empire, wine production received a new round of development. The Romans made improvements in the cultivation of vines and technology have developed new methods of wine production. With the conquest of the Romans the cultivation of grapes spread around the Mediterranean and other regions of Europe and contributed to the development of local winemaking. With the fall of Rome winemaking tradition gradually lost and begin to revive only in the XI century, in a period of rapid economic recovery and strengthening of trade relations of the Italian State. In the second half of the XV century Italian wine again begins to decline. Among the reasons are called the Ottoman conquest in Europe, the destruction of traditional trade relations, the discovery of America and the transfer of the most important commercial centers in Europe on the Atlantic coast, as well as the political fragmentation of Italy and numerous internal armed conflicts. In the late Middle Ages and in modern times were engaged in wine production monastery and small farms, for their own use and sale within the country.

By the middle of the XX century in Italy, along with a wide variety of cultivated grapes, preserved outdated methods of work in the vineyards and wine production technology. Only in 1960, thanks to the clearance at the legislative level regulatory system that guarantees the authenticity and originality of the produced wines ( appellations ), and tightening control over the quality of products, wine-making gets a new impetus to its development. In addition, the production introducing new technologies and the latest research.

Until the mid -1990s Italy held first place in the volume of world wine production. By the beginning of the XXI century Italy ceded primacy winemakers of France.
